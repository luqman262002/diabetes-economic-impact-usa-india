Economic Burden of Diabetes: A Data-Driven Analysis

India vs. United States

By Luqman Bin Fahad

Abstract

Diabetes is a growing global health challenge, placing a significant economic burden on healthcare systems and individuals. This study provides a data-driven analysis of healthcare expenditure trends, comparing India and the United States. By leveraging publicly available datasets, we explore the financial impact of diabetes and predict future healthcare expenditures using statistical models. Our findings highlight disparities in healthcare spending, with policy implications for cost reduction strategies.

Policy Implications

  • Cost Reduction: Governments should focus on subsidizing essential diabetes treatments.
  • Healthcare Investment: Increased spending in India is necessary to bridge the gap with developed nations.
  • Prevention Programs: Policies focused on prevention could mitigate long-term costs.
  • Public Awareness: Early detection and screening programs can help reduce disease burden.

Conclusion

This study highlights the stark differences in healthcare expenditure between India and the USA. While India's spending is growing faster in percentage terms, the absolute gap remains large. Policy interventions are necessary to ensure affordable diabetes care and sustainable healthcare spending. A multi-faceted approach involving infrastructure, funding, and cost-effective strategies is essential for a more equitable healthcare system.
